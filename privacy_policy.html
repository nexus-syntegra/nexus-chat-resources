Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 17, 2026

Nexus Chat ("the App") is developed by Nexus Syntegra. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our App.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Local Data (Stored on Your Device Only)

The following data is stored and processed exclusively on your device and is never transmitted to any server:

  • Conversations: All chat messages and conversation history are stored locally in an encrypted database. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly use the backup feature.
  • Settings & Preferences: Your app configuration and preferences.
  • AI Models: Downloaded language models stored locally for on-device inference.
  • Voice Data: Voice input is processed on-device using local speech recognition. Audio is never transmitted to external servers.
  • Camera & Photos: Images used with vision model features are processed entirely on-device. Images are never transmitted to external servers unless you are using a remote AI backend that you have configured.
  • Biometric Data: If you enable app lock, biometric authentication is handled entirely by the Android system. The App never accesses or stores biometric data.
  • Long-Term Memory: The Nexus Memory feature extracts and stores facts from conversations locally in an encrypted database for personalization. This data never leaves your device.
  • Documents: Documents ingested for RAG (PDF, DOCX, XLSX, text) are processed and indexed locally. Document content is never transmitted externally.

1.2 Data Transmitted to Third Parties

The following data may be transmitted when you enable specific optional features:

  • Web Search Queries: When you enable web search, your search queries are sent to the search provider you configure (Brave Search, Tavily, SerpAPI, or Serpstack). Only the search query and optional coarse location are sent, not your conversation history.
  • Google Drive Backup: When you enable cloud backup, your conversations are encrypted with XChaCha20-Poly1305 before upload and stored in your personal Google Drive appDataFolder (hidden from your main Drive).
  • Google Calendar: When you enable Calendar integration, the App accesses your Google Calendar events via the Google Calendar API to provide scheduling assistance. Access is read/write as you direct.
  • Google Tasks: When you enable Tasks integration, the App accesses your Google Tasks via the Google Tasks API. Access is read/write as you direct.
  • Google Contacts: When you enable Contacts integration, the App accesses your contacts via the Google People API for contact lookup. Access is read-only.
  • Google Gmail: When you enable Gmail integration, the App accesses your Gmail via the Gmail API as you direct. The App does not read or scan emails without your explicit request.
  • Smart Home: When you configure smart home integration (Google Home, Home Assistant, or IFTTT), device commands are sent to the respective service. The App does not store smart home device data beyond the current session.
  • Remote AI Backend: If you configure a remote AI backend or LM Studio connection, your messages are sent to that server. This is user-configured and the App does not control these servers.
  • Remote MCP Servers: If you configure remote Model Context Protocol (MCP) servers, tool calls and their results are exchanged with those servers. This is user-configured.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: When enabled (default: on), anonymized crash reports are sent to Google Firebase. These include app version, device model, OS version, and crash stack traces. No conversation content is included. You can disable this in Settings > Security > Crash Reports & Analytics.
  • Firebase Analytics: When enabled (default: on), anonymized usage patterns (e.g., feature usage frequency, session duration) are sent to Google Firebase. No conversation content or personal data is included. You can disable this in Settings > Security > Crash Reports & Analytics.
  • HuggingFace: Model files are downloaded from HuggingFace repositories. Only the model download request (URL and file metadata) is sent; no user data or conversation content is transmitted.
  • Inference Engine Updates: The on-device inference engine (llama.cpp) ships inside the installed APK by default, and in the default state no requests are made for engine updates. If you opt into an updatable engine channel in Settings > System > Inference Engine Version, the App additionally (a) fetches a manifest of available builds from raw.githubusercontent.com/nexus-syntegra/nexus-chat-resources (cached for up to one hour), and (b) downloads the specific build you select from github.com/nexus-syntegra/nexus-chat-resources/releases. All requests are over HTTPS and include only standard HTTP metadata (IP address, user agent) that GitHub logs for any public request; no account identifier, device identifier, or tracking token is added, and no telemetry about which version you picked is sent to any server we control. Every downloaded build is verified against a SHA-256 hash published in the signed manifest before it is loaded. You can revert to the built-in engine at any time via Settings > System > Inference Engine Version > Revert to pinned.
  • Google Play Integrity: The App uses the Google Play Integrity API to verify device and app integrity. Device integrity signals are sent to Google as part of this verification. No user data or conversation content is included.
  • ML Kit: The App uses Google ML Kit for on-device translation and language identification. ML Kit may download language models from Google servers. Translation is performed on-device; text is not sent to Google.
  • Google Play Billing: If you make in-app purchases, purchase transactions are processed by Google Play. The App receives purchase status but does not process payment information directly.

1.3 Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect advertising identifiers (we explicitly opt out of the advertising ID)
  • We do not collect precise (GPS) location data
  • We do not collect or transmit conversation content to our servers
  • We do not sell, trade, or share any user data with data brokers or advertisers

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information solely to provide the App's functionality:

  • To enable AI chat conversations using local models
  • To save and restore your conversation history
  • To provide web search results when requested
  • To enable cloud backup when you choose to use it
  • To provide personal assistant features (Calendar, Tasks, Contacts, Gmail, Smart Home) when you enable them
  • To identify and fix crashes and improve app stability (via Firebase Crashlytics, when enabled)
  • To understand anonymized usage patterns and improve the app experience (via Firebase Analytics, when enabled)
  • To verify device and app integrity for security purposes

3. Data Security

  • All AI inference happens on-device using local models
  • Sensitive data (API keys, credentials) is stored using Android's EncryptedSharedPreferences with AES-256-GCM encryption, backed by hardware-secured Android Keystore
  • Conversation data is stored in a locally encrypted SQLCipher database using AES-256 encryption with hardware-backed key management via Android Keystore
  • Google Drive backups are encrypted with XChaCha20-Poly1305 before upload and stored in a private appDataFolder
  • All network connections to third-party APIs use HTTPS with certificate pinning
  • The App employs runtime application self-protection (RASP) to detect tampering, rooting, hooking frameworks, and other security threats
  • Remote features are automatically disabled if device integrity is compromised

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information. Data is only shared when:

  • You explicitly enable web search (queries sent to your chosen provider)
  • You explicitly enable cloud backup (encrypted data sent to your Google Drive)
  • You explicitly enable Google service integrations (data exchanged with Google APIs)
  • You explicitly enable smart home integrations (commands sent to your configured service)
  • You configure a remote AI backend or MCP servers (data sent to your specified servers)
  • Crash reports and analytics are enabled (anonymized data sent to Firebase)

5. Data Retention

  • Conversations & Local Data: Stored on your device until you delete them or uninstall the App.
  • Google Drive Backups: Stored in your Google Drive until you delete them. The App does not automatically delete backups.
  • Crash Reports: Firebase Crashlytics retains crash data for 90 days.
  • Analytics: Firebase Analytics retains data per Google's standard retention policy (up to 14 months for aggregated data).
  • Search Queries: Not retained by the App after the search session. Retention by the search provider is governed by their privacy policy.

6. Your Rights and Choices

  • Delete Data: You can delete all app data from Settings > Security > Delete All Data, by clearing the app's storage in Android Settings, or by uninstalling the App.
  • Disable Features: Web search, cloud backup, remote backends, Google integrations, smart home, and crash reporting/analytics are all optional and can be disabled in Settings.
  • Disable Analytics: You can opt out of crash reports and analytics in Settings > Security > Crash Reports & Analytics.
  • Export Data: You can export your conversations from the App.
  • Revoke Permissions: You can revoke any app permissions (camera, microphone, location, storage) at any time in Android Settings.
  • Revoke Google Access: You can revoke the App's access to Google services at any time via your Google Account security settings.
  • Right to Access: All your data is stored locally on your device and is directly accessible to you.
  • Right to Erasure: You can delete all data at any time as described above.

7. Children's Privacy

The App is not intended for users under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

8. Location Data

The App requests approximate (coarse) location permission only. Location data is:

  • Used only to enrich web search queries with geographic context when you enable this feature
  • Never stored persistently by the App
  • Never transmitted beyond the search query to your configured search provider
  • Never used for tracking, profiling, or advertising
